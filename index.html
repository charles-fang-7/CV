Selected Projects

AR Guided Teleop Surgery

ARCLab, UCSD
San Diego, US
July 2018 - Present
Time delay is a major problem in remote teleop surgery. There can be a delay around 1 sec between the real scene and the surgeon's view. To resolve this, we propose to use AR to predict the position of the tooltips. Basically, we render "fake" tools at the position where the actual tools would go to according to the surgeon's motion. I participated in buiding an EKF for realtime transformation tracking, conducting a successful user study, and experimental evaluation.

Unsupervised Representation Learning

CVML Lab, SJTU
Shanghai, China
April 2018 - June 2018
We propose an unsupervised/self-supervised learning method for vision tasks. Essentially, it lets a model learn by separating mixed-up images. We evaluate our method via transfer learning performance, where it yields satisfactory results comparing to previous methods.

Image Style Transfer

CVML Lab, SJTU
Shanghai, China
July 2017 - November 2017
We propose a non-parametric, non-iterative style transfer pipeline especially for photorealistic image pairs having similar structures. With simple annotations of structure and semantics, either given by other algorithms or by human, our method is able to generate photorealitic, semantic-coherent and robust results in less than 20 seconds, while previous photorealistic methods take minutes or yield unpredictable artifacts.
